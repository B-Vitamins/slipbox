*For the case of two attractive* \(\delta\) -*function potentials, namely,*

\[
V(x)=c \delta\left(x+\frac{a}{2}\right)+c \delta\left(x-\frac{a}{2}\right)
\]

*with* \(c<0\), *find the energy splitting* \(\Delta E\) *between the two bound states for a large separation* \(a\). *You should express* \(\Delta E\) *in terms of* \(a\) *and the bound state* \(E_{0}\) *of a single* \(\delta\) -*function potential.*

\begin{align*}
V(x)=c \delta\left(x+\frac{a}{2}\right)+c \delta\left(x-\frac{a}{2}\right)
\end{align*}

The potential is reflection symmetric, so the solutions are bound to be odd/even. Outside the influence of the wells, solutions are plane waves.

The even solutions are:

\begin{align*}
\psi_{E} \left( x \right) =
\begin{cases}
A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa x  \right \rbrace \quad &\left( x < \frac{a}{2} \right) \\
B \left( \exp \left \lbrace + \kappa x  \right \rbrace + \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa x  \right \rbrace  \right)  \quad &\left( -\frac{a}{2} < x < \frac{a}{2} \right) \\
A \exp \left \lbrace \kappa x  \right \rbrace \quad  &\left( x < - \frac{a}{2} \right)
\end{cases}
\end{align*}

Impose continuity constraints at \(x = a/2\):

\begin{align*}
A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a/2  \right \rbrace = B \left( \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a/2 \right \rbrace + \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a/2 \right \rbrace \right) \Longrightarrow A = B \left( \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a \right \rbrace + 1  \right)
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
&D_{x}^{2} \psi \left( a/2 \right) = - \dfrac{2mE_{0}}{\hbar^2} \psi \left( a/2 \right) \Longrightarrow \\
&- \kappa A \exp \left \lbrace  - \kappa a/2  \right \rbrace - B \left( \kappa \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a/2  \right \rbrace - \kappa \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a /2 \right \rbrace \right) = - \dfrac{2 m E_{0}}{\hbar^2} A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a/2  \right \rbrace \\
&\Longrightarrow \exp \left \lbrace -  \kappa a  \right \rbrace = \dfrac{\hbar^{2} \kappa}{m E_{0}} - 1.
\end{align*}

It's a transcendental equation. A falling exponential and a line erupting from below \(\Re x\) and shooting up and across the first quadrant. It's bound to intersect the falling exponential /somewhere/: *An even bound state is guaranteed.*

The energy is

\begin{align*}
E_{0, \text{even}} = \dfrac{\hbar^{2} \kappa}{1 + \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a \right \rbrace}.
\end{align*}

The odd solution are:

\begin{align*}
\psi_{E} \left( x \right) =
\begin{cases}
A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa x  \right \rbrace \quad &\left( x < \frac{a}{2} \right) \\
B \left( \exp \left \lbrace + \kappa x  \right \rbrace - \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa x  \right \rbrace  \right)  \quad &\left( -\frac{a}{2} < x < \frac{a}{2} \right) \\
-A \exp \left \lbrace \kappa x  \right \rbrace \quad  &\left( x < - \frac{a}{2} \right)
\end{cases}
\end{align*}

Impose continuity constraints at \(x = a/2\):

\begin{align*}
A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace = B \left( \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a  \right \rbrace - \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace \right) \Longrightarrow A = B \left(  \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a  \right \rbrace - 1 \right)
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
&D_{x}^{2} \psi \left( a/2 \right) = - \dfrac{2 m E_{0}}{\hbar^{2}} \psi \left( a/2 \right) \Longrightarrow \\
&- \kappa A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace - B \left( \kappa \exp \left \lbrace \kappa a  \right \rbrace + \kappa \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace \right) = - \dfrac{2 m E_{0}}{\hbar^2} A \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace \\
& \Longrightarrow \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace = 1 - \dfrac{\hbar^{2} \kappa}{m E_{0}}
\end{align*}

It's a transcendetal equation. A falling exponential again but the line down from the \(\Re y> 0\). There will only be a solution if it crosses the falling exponential somewhere. It it lies entirely within the area enclosed by the exponential, then there is the odd solution: *An odd bound solution is /not/ guaranteed.* Thus

\begin{align*}
E_{0} \leq \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a} \Longrightarrow \text{One bound state (even)}
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
E_{0} \geq \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2ma} \Longrightarrow \text{Two bound state (one odd one even)}.
\end{align*}

If it does exist:

\begin{align*}
E_{0, \text{odd}} = \dfrac{\hbar^{2} \kappa}{1 - \exp \left \lbrace - \kappa a  \right \rbrace}.
\end{align*}

The energy split is:

\begin{align*}
\Delta E = E_{0, \text{odd}} - E_{0, \text{even}} = \dfrac{2 \hbar \kappa}{1 - \exp \left \lbrace - 2 \kappa a \right \rbrace}
\end{align*}

*Find the potential \(V(x)\) (assuming that \(V(x) \to 0\) as \(x \to \pm \infty\)), and the bound state energy \(E\).*

The time-independent Schrodinger equation is

\begin{equation*}
\left[ - \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2m} \dfrac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}} + V(x) \right] \thinspace \psi \left( x \right) = E \psi \left( x \right)
\end{equation*}

Substituting \(\psi(x)=\frac{c}{\cosh \left( x/a \right)}\) and solving for \(V(x)\)

\begin{equation*}
V(x) =  E - \dfrac{\hbar^2}{2m} \left[ \dfrac{\sech^{2}(x/a) - \tanh^{2}(x/a)}{a^{2}} \right]
\end{equation*}

Using the identity \(\sech^{2} (x/a) = 1 - \tanh^{2} (x/a)\)

\begin{equation*}
V(x) =  E - \dfrac{\hbar^2}{2m} \left[ \dfrac{1 - 2\tanh^{2}(x/a)}{a^{2}} \right]
\end{equation*}

When \(x \to \pm \infty\), \(\tanh^{2}(x/a) \to 1\).

Thus
\begin{equation*}
\lim_{x \to \pm \infty} \left[ V(x) \right] = E + \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2ma^{2}}
\end{equation*}

For \(V(x) \to 0\) when \(x \to \pm \infty\) we must have

\begin{equation*}
\boxed{
E = - \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}
}
\end{equation*}
Substituting \(E = -\dfrac{\hbar^2}{2 m a^{2}}\) in the expression for \(V(x)\)

\begin{equation*}
\boxed{
V(x) = -\dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{ma^{2} \cosh^{2}(x/a)}
}
\end{equation*}

As a sanity check, constructing the Hamiltonian operator with this \(V(x) = -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{m a^{2} \cosh^{2} (x/a)}\) and checking for the eigenvalue of the eigenvector \(\psi(x) = \frac{c}{\cosh(x/a)}\) yields \(E = - \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}\); same as obtained above.

\begin{align*}
H \psi(x) &= \left[ -\dfrac{\hbar^2}{2m} \dfrac{d^{2}}{dx^{2}} - \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{m a^{2} \cosh^{2} (x/a)} \right] \dfrac{c}{\cosh (x/a)} \\
&= \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}} \left( \dfrac{2c}{\cosh^{3}(x/a)} - \dfrac{c}{\cosh(x/a)}\right) \\
&- \dfrac{c\hbar^{2}}{ma^{2} \cosh^{3}(x/a)} = - \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}} \dfrac{c}{\cosh(x/a)} = E \thinspace \psi (x).
\end{align*}

To /second-order/:

\begin{align*}
E_{n}^{2} &= \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{\left \lvert \left \langle n^{0} \left \lvert V \right \rvert m^{0} \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2}}{E_{n}^{0} - E_{m}^{0}} \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{\left \lvert \left \langle n^{0} \left \lvert (a + a^{\dagger})^{2} \right \rvert m^{0} \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2}}{2(n - m)} \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{\left \lvert \left \langle n^{0} \left \lvert \left(aa + a a^{\dagger} + a^{\dagger} a + a^{\dagger} a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert m^{0} \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2}}{2(n - m)} \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{\left \langle n^{0} \left \lvert \left(aa + a a^{\dagger} + a^{\dagger} a + a^{\dagger} a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert m^{0} \right \rangle \left(\left \langle n^{0} \left \lvert \left(aa + a a^{\dagger} + a^{\dagger} a + a^{\dagger} a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert m^{0} \right \rangle\right)^{\ast}}{2(n - m)} \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{[n^{1/2} (m+1)^{1/2} \left \langle n-1 \vert m + 1 \right \rangle + (n+1)^{1/2} m^{1/2} \left \langle n+1 \vert m-1 \right \rangle]}{2(n - m)} \times \\
&[m^{1/2} (n+1)^{1/2} \left \langle m-1 \vert n + 1 \right \rangle + (m+1)^{1/2} n^{1/2} \left \langle m+1 \vert n-1 \right \rangle] \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \sum_{m}^{\prime} \dfrac{[n (m+1) \left \langle n-1 \vert m + 1 \right \rangle \left \langle m+1 \vert n-1 \right \rangle + (n+1) m \left \langle n+1 \vert m-1 \right \rangle \left \langle m-1 \vert n + 1 \right \rangle]}{2(n - m)} \\
&= \dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{2\omega^3 m^2} \left[ n \left( n-1 \right)/2 - (n+1) (n+2)/2 \right] = -\dfrac{\lambda^{2} \hbar}{\omega^3 m^2} \left( n + \dfrac{1}{2} \right).
\end{align*}

Writing the /exact/ expression for \(E_{n}\)

\begin{align*}
E = \hbar \sqrt{\omega^2 + \dfrac{2 \lambda}{m}} \left( n + \dfrac{1}{2} \right), \qquad n = 0, \thinspace 1, \thinspace 2, \thinspace \dotso.
\end{align*}

as a Taylor series in \(\lambda\) about \(\lambda = 0\):

\begin{align*}
E = \hbar \omega \left( n + \dfrac{1}{2} \right) + \left[ \dfrac{\hbar}{m \omega} \left( n + \dfrac{1}{2} \right) \right] \lambda - \left[\dfrac{\hbar}{m^{2} \omega^{3}} \left( n + \dfrac{1}{2} \right)\right] \lambda^{2} + \dotso
\end{align*}

we put on show that

\begin{align*}
E_{n} = E_{n}^{0} + E_{n}^{1} \lambda + E_{n}^{2} \lambda^{2} + \dotso.
\end{align*}

which should satisfy the ask.

*Consider a one-dimensional problem with a Hamiltonian given by*

\begin{align*}
H=\frac{p^{2}}{2 m}+V(x),
\end{align*}

*where* \(x\) *and* \(p\) *are operators. Let us denote the eigenstates and eigenvalues of* \(H\) *by \(|n\rangle\) *and* \(E_{n}\) *respectively; we will assume that* \(E_{n}\) *only takes a discrete set of values.*
** SOLVED Part I
CLOSED: [2022-11-21 Mon 01:17]
*Calculate the double commutator* \([x,[H, x]]\).

\begin{align*}
\left[ x, \left[ H, x \right] \right] &= \left[ x, \left[ p^{2}/2m + V(x), x \right] \right] \\
&= \left[ x, \left[ p^{2}/2m, x \right] \right] \\
&= \left[ x, p \left[ p/2m, x \right] + \left[ p/2m, x \right] p \right] \xrightarrow[]{\left[ x, p \right] = i \hbar} \\
&= \left[ x, - i \hbar p/ m \right] \\
&= \hbar^{2}/m.
\end{align*}

** SOLVED Part II
CLOSED: [2022-11-21 Mon 01:17]
*Use this and the fact that the identity operator can be written as the sum* \(I=\sum_{n^{\prime}}\left|n^{\prime}\right\rangle\left\langle n^{\prime}\right|\) *to derive the equation*

\[
\sum_{n^{\prime}}\left(E_{n^{\prime}}-E_{n}\right)\left|\left\langle n^{\prime}|x| n\right\rangle\right|^{2}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} .
\]

*This is an example of a 'sum rule'.*

\begin{align*}
S &\equiv \sum_{n^{\prime}} \left( \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle - \left \langle n \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \right) \left \langle n \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle - \left \langle n \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \left \langle n \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \left \langle n \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle  \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle - \left \langle n \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \left \langle n \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n^{\prime} \right \rangle \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \\
&= \left \langle n \left \lvert x H x  \right \rvert n \right \rangle - \left \langle n \left \lvert H  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \left \langle n \left \lvert x^{2}  \right \rvert n \right \rangle \\
&= \dfrac{1}{2} \left \langle n \left \lvert \left[ x, \left[ H, x \right] \right] \right \rvert n \right \rangle  \xrightarrow[]{\left[ x, \left[ H, x \right] \right] = \hbar^{2}/m} \\
&= \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2m}
\end{align*}

** SOLVED Part III
CLOSED: [2022-11-21 Mon 01:11]
*Show that the above sum rule is satisfied for all the eigenstates of* \(H\) *for the case* \(V=(1 / 2) m \omega^{2} x^{2}\).

With \(V=(1 / 2) m \omega^{2} x^{2}\), the Hamiltonian is that of the quantum harmonic oscillator. Recall that for the oscillator,

\begin{align*}
x = \left( \dfrac{\hbar}{2 \omega m} \right)^{1/2} \left( a + a^{\dagger} \right).
\end{align*}

Substituting in the sum immediately furnishes a direct evaluation:

\begin{align*}
S &= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \left( E_{n^{\prime}} - E_{n} \right) \left \lvert \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert x \right \rvert n \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2} \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \left( E_{n^{\prime}} - E_{n} \right) \left \lvert \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert \left( \dfrac{\hbar}{2 \omega m} \right)^{1/2} \left( a + a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert n \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2} \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \hbar \omega \left(n^{\prime} - n \right) \left \lvert \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert \left( \dfrac{\hbar}{2 \omega m} \right)^{1/2} \left( a + a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert n \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2} \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2m} \left(n^{\prime} - n \right) \left \lvert \left \langle n^{\prime} \left \lvert \left( a + a^{\dagger} \right) \right \rvert n \right \rangle  \right \rvert^{2} \xrightarrow[]{a \vert n \rangle = n^{1/2} \vert n-1 \rangle, \thinspace a^{\dagger} \vert n \rangle = \left( n + 1 \right)^{1/2} \vert n + 1 \rangle} \\
&= \sum_{n^{\prime}} \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2m} \left(n^{\prime} - n \right) \left[ \delta_{n,n^{\prime} + 1}n + \delta_{n,n^{\prime}-1} \left( n + 1 \right) \right] \\
&= \dfrac{\hbar^{2}}{2m}.
\end{align*}
