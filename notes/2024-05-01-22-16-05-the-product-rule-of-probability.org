#+TITLE: The product rule of probability
#+FILETAGS: :concept:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

For two events \(A\) and \(B\) within the same probability space, and \(P(B) > 0\), the product rule of probability states

\[P(A, B) = P(A \mid B) P(B).\]

It follows from the definition of the [[id:7214a7da-7048-4c8a-9ab1-a3590a199ccd][Joint probability]] \(P (A, B) \equiv P(A \cap B)\) and the [[id:a2ad9677-e9f8-4558-8984-2e2efe72af3c][Conditional probability]] \( P(A \mid B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)\).
